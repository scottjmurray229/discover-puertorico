## Why Cabo Rojo

Cabo Rojo is Puerto Rico's most underrated destination. While tourists crowd San Juan and Rincón, this southwestern corner delivers some of the island's most dramatic landscapes almost entirely to yourself: red limestone cliffs dropping 200 feet into turquoise water, surreal pink salt flats stretching to the horizon, and beaches so empty you'll wonder if you took a wrong turn.

The lighthouse at Los Morrillos — perched on the cliff edge with Caribbean views in every direction — is the single best viewpoint in Puerto Rico. And when you're done with nature, the fishing village of Boquerón has the island's best seafood strip: a row of open-air restaurants serving oysters, whole fried fish, and $3 Medalla beers.

## Things to Do

**Los Morrillos Lighthouse** — Drive or hike to this 1882 lighthouse at the southwestern tip of Puerto Rico. The red limestone cliffs are stunning. Below the lighthouse, Playa Sucia (Playuela) is one of the island's best beaches — a short, steep trail down. Free.

**Salt Flats** — The Cabo Rojo Wildlife Refuge protects hundreds of acres of salt pans that range from white to pink depending on the season. Walk the interpretive trails, spot migratory birds, and photograph one of the most surreal landscapes in the Caribbean. Free.

**Boquerón Beach** — The town beach. Wide, clean, and well-maintained with bathrooms, showers, and food vendors. $5 parking on weekends. Popular with local families.

**Playa Buyé** — Hidden gem. A palm-fringed crescent of calm water accessed through a residential area. Minimal crowds, no facilities. Bring everything you need.

**Joyuda Seafood Strip** — A stretch of waterfront restaurants on Route 102 serving the freshest seafood on the island. Pull up to any one and order the chillo frito entero (whole fried snapper, $18-25). Eat overlooking the water.

## Where to Eat

**Annie's Seafood** — On the Joyuda strip. The mofongo relleno de mariscos (mashed plantain stuffed with seafood) is worth the drive from anywhere on the island. $15-25.

**Boquerón Oyster Bars** — Walk the strip in Boquerón town and try oysters ($1-2 each) from multiple vendors. Each one shucks them fresh to order. Add lime and hot sauce.

**El Bohío** — Waterfront restaurant in Joyuda with massive portions. The asopao de mariscos (seafood stew) is the best on the southwest coast. $12-20.

## Scott's Pro Tips

<div class="scott-tips">

**Getting Around** — Car essential. The main attractions are spread across 15-20 miles of coastline. Roads are good but winding. Fill up on gas in town — there aren't many stations near the lighthouse.

**Sun Protection** — The salt flats and lighthouse area have zero shade. Bring a hat, sunscreen, and plenty of water. The reflected sun off the salt flats is intense.

**Best Time** — December through May for dry weather. The salt flats are pinkest from February to April. Weekdays are significantly less crowded at the lighthouse.

**Boquerón Nightlife** — On weekends, Boquerón town comes alive with street vendors, music, and cheap drinks. It's the best local nightlife scene outside San Juan.
