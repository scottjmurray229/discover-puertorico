## Why Vieques

Vieques is what happens when the US Navy leaves and nature reclaims the land. For 60 years, two-thirds of this island was a military base. When the Navy departed in 2003, it left behind miles of undeveloped coastline, pristine reefs, and the kind of empty Caribbean beaches that don't exist anywhere else. The former military land is now a national wildlife refuge — no development allowed.

Then there's Mosquito Bay. Certified by Guinness as the brightest bioluminescent bay in the world, it contains up to 700,000 dinoflagellates per gallon of water. On a dark night, every paddle stroke lights up electric blue. Every fish leaves a glowing trail. It's science fiction brought to life.

## Things to Do

**Mosquito Bay Bio Tour** — The island's crown jewel. Book a kayak or electric boat tour ($55-65) on a moonless night. The water erupts in blue-green light with every movement. This is a once-in-a-lifetime experience. Book 2-3 weeks in advance for new moon dates.

**Sun Bay Beach** — The most accessible beach on the island. A wide crescent of golden sand with calm water, bathrooms, and shade trees. $5 parking. Perfect for families.

**Playa Negra (Black Sand Beach)** — A rare black sand beach formed from volcanic rock. Small and dramatic. The water is calm and clear for snorkeling.

**Former Military Beaches** — The real treasures. Playa Caracas (Red Beach), Playa La Plata (Blue Beach), and Pata Prieta (Secret Beach) are all inside the wildlife refuge. Dirt roads, no facilities, no crowds. Bring everything you need.

**Wild Horse Watching** — Over 2,000 wild Paso Fino horses roam freely. You'll see them on the roads, on the beaches, and grazing in fields. They're docile but keep your distance.

## Where to Eat

**El Quenepo** — Vieques' best restaurant. Creative Caribbean cuisine with local ingredients on a candlelit patio. The whole snapper and coconut-crusted shrimp are excellent. $25-45 per entrée. Reservations essential.

**Bili** — Modern tapas bar in Isabel Segunda. Great cocktails, shareable plates, good vibe. $15-25 per person.

**La Tienda Verde** — Breakfast and lunch spot with excellent coffee, fresh juices, and avocado toast. $8-14.

## Scott's Pro Tips

<div class="scott-tips">

**Getting There** — The ferry is cheap ($2.50) but unreliable and books up. Cape Air flights ($80-120) from SJU are 25 minutes and worth the money. Book round-trip.

**Rental Car** — Book a Jeep or SUV before you arrive. There are maybe 6 rental agencies on the island and they sell out. Don't expect Enterprise-level operations — these are small local shops.

**Bio Bay Tips** — New moon = brightest. Full moon = skip it. Don't wear bug spray or sunscreen — the chemicals kill the dinoflagellates. Wear a swimsuit under your clothes.

**Beach Access** — The refuge beaches close at sunset. Some require 4WD (especially Blue Beach after rain). Bring water, snacks, and trash bags — there are no facilities.

**Safety** — Very safe island with low crime. The roads are dark at night (no streetlights) — drive carefully. Watch for horses on the road.
