## Why Fajardo

Fajardo is where Puerto Rico's eastern adventures begin. Sitting on the northeastern tip of the island, this port town is the launching pad for ferries to Vieques and Culebra, boat trips to uninhabited offshore cays, bioluminescent kayak tours, and some of the best snorkeling on the main island.

It's not glamorous — Fajardo is a working town, not a resort destination. But it's perfectly positioned: El Yunque is 20 minutes south, the island ferries leave from Ceiba 15 minutes away, and three marinas offer every kind of boat excursion imaginable.

## Things to Do

**Laguna Grande Bio Bay** — Puerto Rico's second-brightest bioluminescent bay, accessible without a ferry ride. Kayak tours ($45-55) paddle through mangrove channels into the glowing lagoon. New moon nights are brightest. Book 1-2 weeks ahead.

**Icacos Island** — An uninhabited cay 20 minutes offshore with powder-white sand and crystal-clear snorkeling. Day trips by catamaran ($65-85) include gear, drinks, and beach time. The water is absurdly clear.

**Seven Seas Beach** — The best public beach in eastern Puerto Rico. A calm, crescent-shaped beach with facilities, camping, and great snorkeling at the southern end. $5 parking.

**Las Cabezas de San Juan Nature Reserve** — A 316-acre peninsula with seven ecosystems, from mangroves to dry forest. Guided tours ($12) include the 19th-century lighthouse and bioluminescent lagoon viewing. Reservations required.

## Where to Eat

**La Estación** — Casual seafood and Puerto Rican dishes in a renovated gas station. Great fish tacos and craft cocktails. $14-22.

**Pasión por el Fogón** — Upscale Puerto Rican cuisine that's worth a special trip. Chef Myrta's tasting menus showcase local ingredients with creative flair. $40-60/person. Reservations essential.

**Las Vistas Café** — El Conquistador resort area. Fresh Caribbean cuisine with panoramic ocean views. Perfect for sunset dinner. $20-35.

## Scott's Pro Tips

<div class="scott-tips">

**Logistics** — Use Fajardo as a 2-3 night base. Day 1: El Yunque. Day 2: island boat trip or bio bay. Day 3: ferry to Vieques or Culebra. It's the most efficient way to see eastern Puerto Rico.

**Ferry Tips** — Book Ceiba-Vieques and Ceiba-Culebra ferries at porferry.com as far ahead as possible. They sell out. Arrive 1 hour early. Bring cash for parking ($3-5).

**Bio Bay** — Don't put on bug spray or sunscreen before the tour — chemicals kill the dinoflagellates. Wear long sleeves and pants instead.

**Car** — Essential. Fajardo has no meaningful public transit and attractions are spread out. Several car rental agencies operate from the nearby marinas.
