## Why Rincón

Rincón is Puerto Rico's soul. This small west coast town put itself on the map when it hosted the 1968 World Surfing Championship, and it's been attracting wave riders, sunsetters, and free spirits ever since. But you don't need to surf to love it here.

The town sits at the westernmost point of Puerto Rico, where the Caribbean Sea meets the Atlantic Ocean. That geography gives it two gifts: the best surf breaks on the island and the most jaw-dropping sunsets you'll see anywhere. Between January and March, humpback whales migrate through the Mona Passage, breaching and spouting within sight of shore.

The vibe is permanently relaxed. Yoga studios sit next to taco shops. Craft breweries open onto ocean views. Nobody's in a hurry.

## What Are the Top Things to Do in Rincón?

**Surf Maria's Beach** — The most famous wave in Puerto Rico. A reef break that produces clean, consistent waves from November through April. Intermediate to advanced. Boards available for rent at multiple shops ($30-40/day).

**Steps Beach Snorkeling** — When the surf is flat (summer months), Steps Beach becomes a world-class snorkeling spot. Crystal-clear water, sea turtles, and coral formations. Free access down a staircase — hence the name.

**Whale Watching** — January through March, humpback whales pass through the Mona Passage. Watch from the cliffs near the lighthouse or book a boat tour ($65/person) for a closer look.

**Rincón Lighthouse (El Faro)** — The old lighthouse park at Punta Higüero is the best sunset spot on the island. Get there 30 minutes before sunset, bring a drink, and watch the sky explode. Free.

**Craft Beer Trail** — Rincón has a surprising craft beer scene. Rincón Beer Company and Island Brewing Co. both brew excellent tropical IPAs and stouts with ocean views. $6-8 per pint.

## What Should I Eat in Rincón?

**The Pool Bar Sushi** — Yes, a sushi restaurant at a pool bar in rural Puerto Rico. And it's genuinely excellent. Fresh fish, creative rolls, casual atmosphere. $18-30 per person.

**Estela** — Farm-to-table dining with a menu that changes based on what's growing. The most ambitious restaurant in Rincón. Reservations recommended. $35-50/person.

**Jack's Shack** — Beachside bar and grill at Steps Beach. Cold beer, great burgers, sunset views. $12-18. Cash only.

**La Copa Llena at the Black Eagle** — Sunday brunch destination. Excellent coffee, creative egg dishes, and a hilltop setting. $15-22 for brunch.

## Scott's Pro Tips

<div class="scott-tips">

**Getting Around** — You need a car in Rincón. Period. The town is spread out along the coast with no public transit. Rent at BQN airport or drive from San Juan.

**Best Time** — Winter (Nov-Apr) for surf and whale watching. Summer for snorkeling, swimming, and lower prices. The town empties out in September and October.

**Surf Etiquette** — Maria's Beach has a local crew who've been surfing there for decades. Respect the lineup, don't drop in, and you'll be fine. Beginners should take a lesson at a beach break first.

**Sunset Ritual** — Every evening, half the town gathers at the lighthouse or a west-facing bar to watch the sunset. Join them. It never gets old.

**Packing** — Reef-safe sunscreen is required at Steps Beach (it's a marine reserve). Bring water shoes for rocky entries and a light layer for breezy evenings.
