## Why Culebra

Culebra exists to make you question every beach you've ever loved. Flamenco Beach — its crown jewel — is a mile-long horseshoe of white sand so fine it squeaks underfoot, meeting water so clear you can count the fish from shore. It consistently tops global "best beach" lists, and unlike many famous beaches, it actually lives up to the hype.

But Culebra is more than one beach. This tiny island (7 miles long, 4 miles wide) has dozens of coves, excellent snorkeling with resident sea turtles, and an uninhabited offshore island you can kayak to. The pace of life here is deliberately slow — no chain restaurants, no traffic lights, no high-rises.

## Things to Do

**Flamenco Beach** — Go early (before 10 AM) for the best parking and calmest water. Walk west to find the rusted WWII Sherman tanks half-buried in the sand — among the most photographed spots in Puerto Rico. Bring your own shade; umbrella rentals are $20.

**Tamarindo Beach** — The best snorkeling on the island. A sheltered cove where hawksbill and green sea turtles feed on seagrass. Free access, no facilities. Bring your own snorkel gear.

**Culebrita Island** — Kayak or water taxi to this uninhabited cay. Hike to the abandoned 19th-century lighthouse for panoramic views. The natural tidal pools ("The Baths") are perfect for a swim. Half-day trip.

**Zoni Beach** — The local's beach on the eastern end. Wilder, windier, and almost empty. Leatherback sea turtles nest here from April to July. Spectacular at sunset.

## Where to Eat

**Susie's** — The island's institution. Puerto Rican home cooking on a porch overlooking the harbor. The mofongo is among the best on any island. $12-20. Cash only.

**Zaco's Tacos** — Exactly what the name says. Great fish tacos and cold beer on a hilltop with a view. $8-14. A perfect sunset spot.

**Dinghy Dock** — Waterfront bar and restaurant in the harbor. Fresh seafood, strong drinks, and a deck over the water. Live music some weekends. $15-25.

## Scott's Pro Tips

<div class="scott-tips">

**Getting There** — Fly if you can afford it. The ferry is $2.50 but sells out days ahead and has a history of cancellations. Vieques Air Link and Air Flamenco fly from SJU and Ceiba.

**Golf Cart** — The way to get around. Reserve in advance ($60-80/day). The island has one gas station. Roads are rough but navigable.

**Packing** — Bring everything. Culebra has one small grocery store and one pharmacy. Sunscreen, snorkel gear, first aid kit, and enough cash (many places don't take cards).

**Turtle Season** — April through July, leatherback turtles nest on Zoni and Brava beaches. If you see a nesting turtle, keep 30 feet back and don't use flash photography.

**Water** — Culebra occasionally has water shortages. Don't waste it. Fill reusable bottles from your accommodation.
