## Why Arecibo

Arecibo is where Puerto Rico reveals its wild, geological side. The northern coast here is dramatic — limestone karst formations, towering cliffs, and some of the most impressive cave systems in the Western Hemisphere. This is not beach Puerto Rico. This is adventure Puerto Rico.

The region is anchored by two landmarks: the Río Camuy Cave Park, where underground rivers have carved enormous chambers you can walk through, and Cueva Ventana, a cave that opens to a picture-window view of a river valley below. Add the legacy of the Arecibo Observatory — which for decades was the world's largest radio telescope — and you have one of the most unique day trips on the island.

## Things to Do

**Cueva Ventana** — The "Window Cave." A guided hike through a cave system in the karst hills opens to a natural window overlooking the Río Grande de Arecibo valley. The view is stunning and the photos are iconic. $20/person, tours hourly. Wear closed-toe shoes.

**Río Camuy Cave Park** — One of the largest cave systems in the Western Hemisphere. A tram takes you down into a massive sinkhole, then guided tours walk you through chambers filled with stalactites, stalagmites, and an underground river. $18/adult. Allow 2 hours.

**Arecibo Observatory** — The 1,000-foot dish collapsed in 2020 after 57 years of operation, but the visitor center remains open. Interactive exhibits cover SETI, pulsars, and the telescope's role in films like Contact and GoldenEye. $12 admission. A new facility is being planned.

**Arecibo Lighthouse & Historical Park** — Restored 1898 lighthouse with a small museum, mini-zoo, playground, and coastal views. Popular with families. $12/adult.

**Playa Peña Blanca** — Hidden beach at the base of white limestone cliffs. Accessed via a steep trail from the road. No facilities but dramatic scenery and usually empty.

## Where to Eat

**Salitre Meson Costero** — Oceanfront restaurant serving excellent seafood with dramatic cliff views. The mofongo de pulpo (octopus) is outstanding. $15-28.

**La Casa del Mar** — Casual waterfront spot in Arecibo town. Fresh fish, cold beer, and ocean breezes. $12-20.

**Lechoneras on Route 10** — Head south on Route 10 toward Utuado for roadside lechoneras (roast pig stands). Whole pig slow-roasted over charcoal, served with rice, beans, and morcilla. $8-12 for a heaping plate. Peak on weekends.

## Scott's Pro Tips

<div class="scott-tips">

**Getting Around** — Car essential. The caves, observatory, and coast are spread across 20+ miles. Roads in the karst country are winding but well-paved.

**Cave Tips** — Both cave tours involve walking on uneven, sometimes slippery surfaces. Wear closed-toe shoes with grip. The caves are cooler than outside (72-76°F) — bring a light layer. Flash photography is restricted in Río Camuy to protect the bat colonies.

**Combine with Utuado** — Drive south on Route 10 to Utuado for the Taíno indigenous ceremonial site at Caguana ($5) and spectacular mountain scenery. Makes a great full-day loop.

**Best Time** — Morning visits to the caves are less crowded. Cueva Ventana's light through the window is best around midday. The observatory is worth visiting rain or shine since most exhibits are indoor.
