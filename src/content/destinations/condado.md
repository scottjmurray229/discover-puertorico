## Why Condado

Condado is where San Juan goes upscale without losing its Caribbean soul. This narrow strip of land between the Atlantic Ocean and Condado Lagoon packs more good restaurants per square block than anywhere else on the island. Ashford Avenue is the spine — a palm-lined boulevard where designer boutiques sit next to local coffee shops and Art Deco buildings glow in the tropical light.

The beach here is calmer than Isla Verde's — a protected crescent of golden sand perfect for swimming. And when the sun goes down, Condado transforms into Puerto Rico's most walkable dining and nightlife district.

## What Are the Top Things to Do in Condado?

**Condado Beach** — The main draw. Calm, clear water with lifeguards. The western end near the Vanderbilt is less crowded. Beach chairs and umbrella rentals available ($15-20).

**Ashford Avenue** — Window-shop designer boutiques, duck into art galleries, and café-hop your way from one end to the other. Best in the late afternoon when the heat breaks.

**Condado Lagoon** — Rent a paddleboard or kayak ($25/hour) and explore the mangrove-lined lagoon. At night, the bioluminescence occasionally puts on a show.

**La Ventana al Mar** — Small oceanfront park at the eastern end of Condado. Great for sunset watching with food trucks and live music on weekends.

## What Should I Eat in Condado?

**1919** — The Vanderbilt's fine dining restaurant. Chef Juan José Cuevas creates modern Puerto Rican tasting menus that rival anything in New York. $120 per person with wine pairing. Reservations essential.

**Via Appia** — Casual Italian on Ashford that's been a local favorite for decades. Great pizza and pasta in the $14-22 range. Always busy, never disappointing.

**Cocina Abierta** — Chef Martín Louzao's intimate bistro with a constantly changing menu based on what's fresh. Everything is shareable. Budget $40-60 per person.

**Kasalta Bakery** — The Obama-approved bakery (he really did eat here). Famous for their mallorcas (sweet bread sandwiches) and café con leche. $8-12 for breakfast.

## Scott's Pro Tips

<div class="scott-tips">

**Getting Around** — Everything in Condado is walkable. For Old San Juan, it's a 10-minute Uber or a scenic 30-minute waterfront walk. Parking is brutal — don't rent a car if you're staying here.

**Best Time to Visit** — Same as all of San Juan: December through April. Summer brings afternoon thunderstorms but they pass quickly and prices drop 30-40%.

**Money** — US dollars everywhere. Credit cards accepted at all restaurants and hotels. Some beach vendors are cash-only.

**Safety** — One of the safest neighborhoods on the island. Well-lit, well-patrolled, and busy with tourists and locals at all hours.
