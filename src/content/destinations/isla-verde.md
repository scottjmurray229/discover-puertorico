## Why Isla Verde

If you came to Puerto Rico for the beach, Isla Verde is where you start. This Blue Flag-certified stretch of golden sand is consistently rated among the best urban beaches in the Caribbean — wide enough that it never feels crowded, with water that ranges from calm swimming pools to decent surf breaks depending on the season.

Isla Verde has a more resort-oriented vibe than Condado. The big hotels line the beach, water sports operators set up every morning, and the pace is decidedly relaxed. It's also the cheapest of the three San Juan beach neighborhoods and the closest to the airport.

## Things to Do

**Isla Verde Beach** — The main event. Over a mile of golden sand with sections for every mood — calm swimming near the Marriott, body surfing near the Ritz, and quieter stretches at either end. Free public access at multiple points.

**Water Sports** — Jet skiing ($80/30 min), parasailing ($75), stand-up paddleboarding ($30/hour), and surfing lessons ($65) are all available directly on the beach.

**Piñones** — Drive or Uber 10 minutes east to this beachside strip of food kiosks. Alcapurrias (fried fritters stuffed with crab or beef), bacalaítos (cod fritters), and fresh coconut water straight from the machete. $2-5 per item. This is where locals eat.

**Pine Grove Beach** — The quieter, more local end of Isla Verde. Calmer water, fewer tourists, palm tree shade. Popular with families on weekends.

## Where to Eat

**Metropol** — Cuban-Puerto Rican diner that's been serving enormous portions since the 1960s. The carne frita con tostones ($16) feeds two people. Cash preferred.

**Lote 23** — Food truck park in Santurce, a quick Uber ride away. Dozen different vendors from tacos to sushi to craft cocktails. Great atmosphere on weekend nights.

**El Alambique Beach Bar** — Feet-in-the-sand dining with surprisingly good food. Fresh ceviche ($14), cold Medalla beers ($3), and sunset views.

## Scott's Pro Tips

<div class="scott-tips">

**Getting Around** — Isla Verde is linear — everything is along one main road. Walking distance between hotels is easy. For Old San Juan or Condado, use Uber ($10-20).

**Beach Tips** — The beach has no natural shade. Bring or rent an umbrella. The sand gets scorching hot midday — bring sandals. Rip currents can be strong at the eastern end; swim near lifeguard stations.

**Best Time** — Winter months (Dec-Apr) have the best weather and calmest water. Summer has bigger waves if you surf. Avoid holiday weekends when local families pack the beach.

**Safety** — Very safe during the day. Don't leave valuables unattended on the beach. The area between the hotels is well-lit at night.
